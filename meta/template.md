## TL;DR

One to two sentences. The verdict in plain language plus the single most important caveat.

## What it is

Chemical class, typical dose ranges (and route), mechanism class. Two to four sentences.

## Proposed mechanism

What the intervention is claimed to do at the molecular/cellular level. Confidence label one of:
- **Established** — multiple lines of converging evidence
- **Plausible** — biologically reasonable, partially supported
- **Hypothetical** — proposed but not mechanistically demonstrated

## Evidence ladder

### Invertebrate (T5)

Studies in C. elegans, fly, yeast. Direction of effect, effect size range, replication status across labs. "No data found" if absent.

### Mouse / rat (T3-T4)

- Number of independent labs
- ITP / RP2 status (positive cohort? negative? not tested?)
- Effect size range (median lifespan extension %)
- Sex dependence
- Strain dependence
- Dose dependence

### NHP (T4)

Studies in rhesus, marmoset, etc. Endpoints (biomarker / mortality / healthspan).

### Human (T1-T2)

- RCTs that exist (cite each)
- Endpoints actually measured
- Effect direction and size
- Pre-registration status
- Sample sizes

## Confounds

- Control diet adequacy (mouse studies)
- Baseline mortality of strain (was the strain unusually short-lived?)
- Publication bias signal (is the literature dominated by positive results from one lab?)
- Measurement validity (e.g., biomarker that may not reflect aging)

## Conflict of interest scan

- Industry funding present? Which trials?
- Author equity / commercial ties
- Net effect on tier ratings (per methodology section 5)

## Human translation

What the human data actually shows, separate from the extrapolation. If there is no human data, say so explicitly. If human data is null where animal data was positive, that is itself important.

## Calibrated verdict

The verdict band, 2-3 sentences of rationale, and an explicit comparison to the nearest calibration anchor. Format: "Compared to [anchor], this intervention [stronger/weaker/comparable] because [specific evidence comparison]."

## Confidence interval on verdict

What would move the verdict up? What would move it down? How likely is each within the next 2 years given trials in progress?

## Open questions

Questions that, if resolved, would change the verdict. These should be opened as issues on the repository so they can be tracked and resolved publicly.

## Sources

Every citation. Prefer DOI / PubMed link. Mark paywalled vs open. Mark pre-print vs published. ITP results separately tagged.
