# Cover Letter

Dear Editors,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, **"Where the Longevity Discourse is Systematically Wrong: A Calibrated Synthesis of 38 Aging Interventions"**, for publication in *npj Aging*.

## Why this work, why now

The aging-intervention literature has a structural problem: every intervention is reviewed in isolation, often by partisans, often without commitment to a fixed evidence standard. Public belief diverges from the highest-tier evidence in identifiable, repeated ways — resveratrol, NMN, metformin, time-restricted eating — and there is no single, methodologically pre-committed cross-intervention map to push against the divergence.

This manuscript provides one. Before assessing any specific intervention, I locked a methodology: a six-tier evidence ladder (T0–T5), six verdict bands, explicit replication standards, conflict-of-interest discounts, and five external calibration anchors (exercise, caloric restriction, rapamycin, NMN, resveratrol). The methodology and anchors were timestamped on Zenodo (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19768647) on 2026-04-24, prior to per-intervention work. Thirty-eight interventions and four pairwise interactions were then synthesized against the locked rules.

## What is novel

1. **Pre-commitment.** The methodology was frozen and archived publicly before assessments began, making the central anti-bias mechanism externally auditable.
2. **Triangulation against named anchors.** Every verdict is defended against the closest of five calibration anchors. The reasoning is contestable on its own terms, not just by appeal to the literature.
3. **Eight specific findings where the public discourse and the highest-tier evidence diverge** — including the metformin–exercise antagonism, the GLP-1 muscle-loss problem, the time-restricted-eating null, the taurine downgrade, and the structural under-attention to sauna/heat exposure.
4. **Open data.** The full per-intervention database, build scripts, and verdict-table snapshot are CC-BY archived (companion deposit DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19769433).

## Fit with *npj Aging*

The manuscript is a SANRA-formatted narrative review. It is descriptive of *what the evidence currently supports*, not of new biology — but it is calibrated, replicable in approach, and offers a re-usable methodology that other syntheses can adopt or contest. *npj Aging*'s open-access mandate fits the goal of making the verdict map as broadly readable as possible.
